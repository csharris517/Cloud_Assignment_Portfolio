What is Porter's Value Chain Model?

Michael Porter, as mentioned in the assignment Porter's Competitive Model, also developed what is referred to as the Value Chain Model or Generic Value Chain. The aim of this model is to show "how goods and services move through an organization and value is added to them" (Dennis Adams). The model breaks an organization's activities into Support Activities and Primary Activities. Support Activities are also known as "overhead activities". These are the activities that are not directly involved in primary operations but are essential to the organization's functions. Primary Activities are the primary value adding activities for a company.


Primary Activities

Inbound Logistics is considered a primary activity because it involves the obtaining of raw materials in order to produce the organization's goods or services.


Operations is the second primary activity. It involves the organization performing operations on the raw materials in order to add value to them by transforming them into a finished good or service.


Outbound Logistics is the primary activity which involves getting the finished goods or services into inventory and eventually distributed to be sold to stores and/or customers.


Marketing and Sales is the process of marketing a good to the public or organizations in order to increase its sales. This is a primary activity because it increases the good's exposure and thus further adds value.


Service is the final value-adding primary activity and pertains to services performed after the good or service has already been sold or consumed.


Support Activities

Infrastructure can refer to legal infrastructure, accounting infrastructure, or anything that needs to be in place for the organization to perform its normal operations. This support activity is very important because not only does it support the primary activities, but it also helps maintain the other support activities.


Human Resource Management is the function of a business that insures that there are enough and the correct people working for the organization. This support activity affects all primary activities because people must be hired and managed for each activity along the value chain.


Technology Development, in this case, refers both to the Research and Development function of developing new goods and services and the development of IT processes. This is a support activity because assist in the business's primary activities.


Procurement refers to the purchasing of goods along the value chain. While these goods may be necessary in the finished goods or services, they do not directly add value. Thus, Procurement is a support activity.
