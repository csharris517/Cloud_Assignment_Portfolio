As you can see from the illustration above, Cloud Computing affects all activities within an organization, even creating new support and primary activities. Below is how each activity in Porter's Value Chain Model can be impacted by the proliferation of Cloud Computing in business.


Primary Activities

Inbound Logistics, Operations, and Outbound Logistics are all part of an organization's supply chain. Supply chain management has been greatly affected by the introduction of SCM Information Systems that are integrated with the cloud. Using cloud and internet of things technologies, an organization has an automated way of tracking and managing goods as they flow through the value chain. For example, a company may have an Inbound Logistics system in place that automatically places an order using cloud services when raw goods inventory is low. This automation saves time and resources, thus adding more value to the finished goods or services.

Marketing and Sales and Service are not as obviously impacted by Cloud Computing. However, the effects can be of great benefit to the organization. Consider, for instance, cell phone applications. A large portion of many apps' revenue is from advertisements. This mobile/Cloud Computing technology has introduced a new method for Marketing and Sales to reach new potential customers in an automated and direct way.


Support Activities

Procurement is affected by the introduction of Cloud Computing in that it is much easier for organizations to make purchases from other organizations using SaaS (Software as a Service) applications such as some e-commerce websites. Technology Development is the most impacted support activity. Not only do organizations now consider designing their goods and services with IoT technologies, but they should also be designing their internal Information Systems to operate in - or in connection with - the Cloud.

Human Resource Management and Infrastructure are less obviously impacted but have made vast improvements with SaaS Cloud Computing. For example, organizations can purchase entire software suites that handle internal communications including ticket management systems, Human Resource Portals, and communications software such as Skype for Business.
